The Hard Truth

Your cyber policy is probably
not what you think it is

Here are five things most business owners discover too late — usually at claim time.

1

You're likely overpaying

The cyber insurance market has shifted dramatically. 8 in 10 businesses are paying more than they should for coverage that could be restructured at a lower cost and higher limit.

2

Exclusions have multiplied

Policy exclusion clauses have grown 40% longer since 2020. Nation-state attacks, unpatched software, and war exclusions are now standard — and most businesses have no idea they exist in their policy.

3

Ransomware coverage is shrinking

Carriers have quietly added sublimits and waiting periods to ransomware coverage. Your $2M policy may only cover $250K of a ransomware event — buried in the endorsements.

4

The average breach costs $4.88M

That's the IBM 2024 data. The gap between what your policy says it covers and what it actually pays can be the difference between recovering and closing your doors.

5

You renewed without reviewing

You signed last year's renewal in 10 minutes. The threat landscape and the policy language both changed. What worked last year may have gaps this year. Nobody checked.

Ryan Dunn spent years inside the cyber insurance industry — on panels, at summits, behind the mic on podcasts, and in the rooms where coverage decisions get made. He watched the same pattern repeat everywhere: brokers couldn’t read the policies they were selling, clients couldn’t understand what they were buying, and carriers kept adding exclusions nobody noticed.

The analysis was always manual. Always slow. Always expensive. And always inconsistent. A $2M policy with a buried nation-state exclusion that would deny a ransomware claim? Nobody caught it until claim time. A sublimit that capped ransomware recovery at $250K on a $2M policy? Buried in the endorsements.

Every conference, every panel, every late-night conversation with underwriters and CISOs pointed to the same gap: the industry needed a tool that could stress-test a cyber policy the way a penetration tester stress-tests a network. Fast. Thorough. No conflicts of interest.
